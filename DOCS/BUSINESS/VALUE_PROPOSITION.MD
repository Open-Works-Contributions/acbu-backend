# Value Proposition & Use Cases

## Overview

The African Currency Basket Unit (ACBU) is a stablecoin backed by a basket of African currencies, designed to address real problems around currency stability, cross-border payments, and financial inclusion across the continent. It's analogous to the IMF's SDR (Special Drawing Rights) but focused on Africa, debunking the myth that stablecoins must be pegged to the US dollar.

## Primary Target Users

### Pan-African E-commerce Merchants
- Accept payments from customers across multiple countries without forex exposure to any single currency
- Boost profitability by reducing currency conversion costs
- Eliminate need to maintain multiple currency accounts

### Cross-Border Traders
- Intra-African trade ($192B annually) currently suffers from currency conversion costs and settlement delays
- Reduce transaction costs from 5-20% to under 1%
- Faster settlement times (3-5 seconds vs days)

### SMEs Doing Regional Business
- Avoid maintaining multiple currency accounts
- Use a single currency backed by a basket of currencies
- Natural hedge for businesses operating across multiple African countries

### Diaspora Remittances
- $100B+ flows to Africa annually
- Currently expensive for both diaspora senders and local receivers
- Reduce costs from 3-5% to 0.6% (0.3% in + 0.3% out)

## Core Problem It Solves

Unlike USDT/USDC which expose African users to USD strength (making dollar-denominated debt more expensive when local currencies weaken), ACBU:

- **Reduces single-currency risk** for African businesses
- **Creates a "continental currency"** for trade settlement
- **Lowers conversion costs** for intra-African transactions (currently 5-20% fees)
- **Provides stability** without dollarization dependency

## Competitive Advantage over USD Stablecoins

### Currency Sovereignty
- Value tied to African economic performance, not US monetary policy
- Natural hedge for African businesses earning/spending in local currencies
- Potentially easier regulatory acceptance (supporting African currencies vs. facilitating dollarization)

### Cost Advantages
- **Traditional remittances:** 5-8% fees
- **ACBU model:** 0.6% total (0.3% deposit + 0.3% withdrawal)
- **Savings:** 88-93% cheaper than traditional methods

### Stability Characteristics
- Diversification benefit reduces volatility
- Protection from isolated country crises
- Participation in Africa's overall economic growth

## Use Case Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Intra-African Travel/Business
**Nigerian traveler going to Ghana:**
1. Deposits 500,000 NGN via Flutterwave → Receives ~500 ACBU
2. Arrives in Ghana → Withdraws 30,000 GHS via Paystack
3. **Total cost:** ~1% (vs 5-8% traditional remittance/forex)
4. **Time:** Instant on both ends

### Scenario 2: Pan-African E-commerce
**Merchant accepting payments from 10 countries:**
- Receives ACBU from customers across Africa
- No forex exposure to any single currency
- Can withdraw in local currency when needed
- Lower fees than card payments (0.2% vs 2.5%)

### Scenario 3: Diaspora Remittances
**Diaspora in USA sending to Nigeria:**
1. Sends USDC → Instantly converts to ACBU
2. Family in Nigeria receives ACBU in wallet
3. Can withdraw NGN immediately or keep ACBU
4. **Cost:** 0.3% (vs 3-5% traditional)

## Key Benefits Summary

✅ **Reduced forex risk** between African trading partners  
✅ **Natural hedge** for businesses operating across multiple countries  
✅ **Less exposure** to USD policy decisions  
✅ **Diversification benefit** (safer than single currency)  
✅ **Protection** from isolated country crises  
✅ **Stable corridor** for sending money across borders  
✅ **Predictable value** for recipients
